Alzheimer's & Dementia




What is Dimentia?

Dimentia is a group of symptoms that affects mental cognitive tasks such as memory and reasoning.It is a progressive disease that destroys memory and other important mental functions.

Although dementia mainly affects older people, it is not a normal part of ageing.

Causes of Dimentia

Dementia is caused by damage to or loss of nerve cells and their connections in the brain.

Risk factors

  • Age:Increasing age is the greatest known risk factor for Alzheimer's disease.
  • Past head trauma.
  • Lifelong learning and social engagement.
  • Poor sleep patterns such as difficulty falling asleep or staying asleep,.
  • Lifestyle and heart health: Obesity, High blood pressure and Exposure to smoking and drugs.
