Alzheimer's & Dementia




What is Dimentia?

Dimentia is a group of symptoms that affects mental cognitive tasks such as memory and reasoning.It is a progressive disease that destroys memory and other important mental functions.

Although dementia mainly affects older people, it is not a normal part of ageing.

Causes of Dimentia

Dementia is caused by damage to or loss of nerve cells and their connections in the brain.

Risk factors

  • Age:Increasing age is the greatest known risk factor for Alzheimer's disease.
  • Past head trauma.
  • Lifelong learning and social engagement.
  • Poor sleep patterns such as difficulty falling asleep or staying asleep,.
  • Lifestyle and heart health: Obesity, High blood pressure and Exposure to smoking and drugs.

Stages of Dimentia

Understanding each stage can help make these transitions a little easier on you and your loved one

Stage 1: No impairment

In this stage there are no symptoms of cognitive impairment, mental function is normal

Stage 2: Very mild cognitive decline

In this stage, one seems to forget everyday phrases and the location of important objects such as keys.

Stage 3: Mild cognitive decline

In this stage, one seems to be forgetful, have verbal repetition and poor concentration.

Stage 4: Moderate cognitive decline

In this stage, the symptoms are now more noticable: Social withdrawal, Denial, Trouble wit routine tasks and moodiness.

Stage 5: Moderately severe cognitive decline

In this stage,your loved one may need help with basic things like dressing. They may tend to be confused and have memory loss of personal details and current events.

Stage 6:  Severe cognitive decline

In this stage, your loved one is dependent on you. They may even have anxiety, difficulty in sleeping and also have difficulty in recognizing their loved ones.

Stage 7: Very severe cognitive decline

In this stage,your loved one is unable to care for themselves, lives with severe motor and communication impairment, and may lose the ability to speak or walk.

Treatment and care

There is no treatment currently available to cure dementia or to alter its progressive course. .

However, much can be offered to support and improve the lives of people with dementia such as :

  • Enhance communication: By talking with your loved one, maintain eye contact. Speak slowly in simple sentences, and don't rush the response.
  • Engage in Therapy such as art, music and pet therapy.
  • Encourage exercise: Exercise improves strength and balance.
  • Your doctor might prescribe medications to treat other symptoms or conditions, such as depression, sleep disturbances or agitation.
