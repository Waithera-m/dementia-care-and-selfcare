Selfcare

Your ability to care for a loved one with dementia is contingent on your physical and psychological health. There are various techniques that you can use to maintain you wellbeing

Ask for Help

Remember that everyone needs help every now and then and you don't have to do everything yourself. So don't be afraid to ask friends and family for help and to use local and national resources, including support groups

Identify Useful Stress Coping Mechanisms

Taking care of a loved with dementia can at times be frustrating and you may feel discouraged, confused, angry, or lonely. These emotions are normal.

Usesful tips

  • Remind yourself that you are doing everything you can

  • Remember that you are not perfect, noone is, and that is okay

  • Remember that there are events and things that are out of your control

  • Remember to enjoy the peaceful moments that you experience

  • Remember that it is okay to feel sad, powerless, and experience a sense of loss

  • Understand why you have chosen to be a carer

Get Professional Help

Trained mental professionals create safe spaces where you can work through the emotions and stress that you are experiencing. These professionals also create environments in which you can process unexepcted events and plan for all possible outcomes

Source: National Institute on Aging
